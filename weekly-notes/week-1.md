# Week 1 Research Journal

## Why I Started This Research Journey

My professional background is in cloud engineering, platform security, infrastructure automation, and DevSecOps.

As AI systems become increasingly integrated into software engineering workflows, I have become interested in understanding how these systems affect security, governance, and organisational decision-making.

Many organisations are currently focused on the productivity benefits of AI-assisted development. However, I believe equally important questions remain unanswered regarding security risks, human oversight, governance, and long-term resilience.

This repository represents an effort to explore these questions through structured reading, experimentation, and analysis.

---

## Questions I Am Currently Exploring

1. Do AI coding assistants introduce security vulnerabilities into production systems?

2. How should organisations evaluate productivity gains against security risks?

3. What governance mechanisms are necessary before deploying AI-assisted development workflows at scale?

4. How can frontier models be evaluated before deployment into security-sensitive environments?

5. What are the long-term implications of AI-assisted engineering on expertise development and organisational resilience?

---

## Current Research Focus

My primary research interest is:

Evaluating Security Risks in AI-Assisted Software Development Pipelines

I am particularly interested in understanding whether productivity gains from AI-assisted engineering are accompanied by hidden security costs, governance challenges, or increased technical debt.

## Reflection

AI systems are becoming increasingly capable. The challenge is no longer determining whether these systems can improve productivity. The more important question may be whether organisations can deploy them safely, responsibly, and sustainably.

This journal will document my attempt to better understand that challenge.
