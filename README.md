# Frontier AI Security Research Journal

## Purpose

This repository documents my ongoing exploration of AI security, frontier model evaluation, governance, and the societal implications of increasingly capable AI systems.

The goal is to develop a deeper understanding of how advanced AI systems affect security, software engineering, organisational resilience, governance, and public policy.

This journal serves as a public record of:

* Research notes
* Paper reviews
* Experiment ideas
* Literature reviews
* Policy reflections
* Questions for future investigation

## Why This Repository Exists

Many of the most important questions surrounding advanced AI systems do not yet have clear answers.

Examples include:

* How should frontier models be evaluated before deployment?
* What security risks emerge when AI systems become deeply integrated into engineering workflows?
* How should organisations balance productivity gains against security risks?
* What governance mechanisms are necessary for safe AI deployment?
* How can society build resilience against emerging AI-related risks?

This repository documents my attempt to engage seriously with these questions.

## Research Themes

### AI Security

Understanding how advanced AI systems may introduce, amplify, or mitigate security risks.

### Frontier Model Evaluation

Investigating how increasingly capable models should be tested, evaluated, and stress-tested.

### Software Engineering

Exploring how AI systems affect software development, infrastructure management, and operational security.

### Governance

Examining accountability, oversight, and organisational controls for AI deployment.

### Economics & Policy

Investigating how AI affects labour markets, expertise development, productivity, and societal resilience.
